The subject

A decade on set.
21 out of 100.

Luca Bold is an international fashion model with ten years across South Africa, Europe, and Asia — represented by ICE Models Cape Town and Next Management Milan, currently operating out of Shanghai.

He also built Booked. — a service that audits the digital infrastructure of working fashion creatives and builds what needs to exist to close the legibility gap.

The Booked. framework scores five dimensions of a creative's digital presence: machine-readability, social presence, portfolio structure, contact friction, and discoverability. In June 2026, every test in the framework was run against lucabold.com and its associated digital assets. No adjustments. No exceptions.

Without an entity record (JSON-LD schema, Wikidata entry, Knowledge Panel), search systems cannot resolve the subject with confidence and surface unrelated results to fill the gap.

02

Machine-Readability

What a machine reads
from this site:
almost nothing.

The machine-readability audit checks for structured data (JSON-LD Person schema), meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, Twitter card tags, canonical tags, and image alt text. These are the signals that allow search crawlers, AI systems, and casting platforms to determine who someone is, what they do, and how to reach them.

Complete tag inventory from the live scan of lucabold.com:

Title tag Luca Bold — International Fashion Model & Founder of Booked
Meta description ABSENT
JSON-LD schema ABSENT
og:title ABSENT
og:description ABSENT
og:image ABSENT
og:url ABSENT
Twitter card ABSENT
Canonical tag ABSENT
Image alt text ABSENT — all images are base64-encoded WebP with no alt attributes

The title tag is the single piece of parseable identity on the site. Everything else — the agency, the current market, the category, the career credits, the contact path — exists only in the visual layer. A machine reading this site cannot determine that this person is a model, who represents them, or where they work.

A cold AI query — "Who is Luca Bold model?" — returns a basic physical description scraped from agency data. It does not return: current market (Shanghai), current operating agency, notable credits, company (Booked.), or any structured career context. A casting platform running AI-assisted first-pass filtering cannot construct a bookable entity record from available sources.

03

Indexed Dead Pages

Four pages Google
still sends people to.
All of them are dead.

Google has indexed multiple subpages from lucabold.com that return HTTP 404. These pages appear in search results for queries about this model's name and career. Every click terminates with an error — a structural trust failure at the point of discovery.

Beyond user experience, indexed 404s dilute domain authority. The domain is sending three competing signals for a single query: a functional homepage, a dead "fashion model" page, and a dead "blogs" index — all from the same domain, all in the top 10.

The Shanghai blog post is the most significant loss. A post about the experience of being a fashion model in Shanghai would be the single piece of geo-specific content anchoring this model to their current operating market. It is indexed and appears in searches for "Luca Bold Shanghai" — and it returns 404. That search context cannot be owned until the content is recreated and re-indexed.

04

Contact Friction

The contact page
does not exist.

lucabold.com includes a "Contact" link in the navigation. A contact section exists as an in-page anchor (#contact). The URL /contact does not exist. A casting director who receives a link to this site and clicks "Contact" directly reaches a dead page.

More critically: no email address appears anywhere in the extractable content of lucabold.com. No WhatsApp link. No agency reference. A professional encountering this site for the first time has no confirmed route to the subject from the page itself.

Step 1 Land on lucabold.com. No contact information visible in extractable content.
Step 2 Click "Contact" in the navigation.
Step 3 lucabold.com/contact — HTTP 404. The page does not exist.

The in-page anchor (#contact) technically exists and may render contact information in a browser. A crawler, a search engine, and any AI agent reading the page source finds nothing there. The confirmed contact path for a professional who finds this model online: Google → ICE Models website → agency email. A three-step external redirect. No direct approach is possible from the personal site.

A note from the founder

The cobbler's
children
still need shoes.

All gaps are structural. None are personal. The creative's talent is not in question. The infrastructure is.

I built Booked. because I watched talented people lose work to people with less talent and better infrastructure. I built the framework. I built the rubric. I built the scoring system.

Then I ran it on myself.

The score is 21 out of 100. My personal site ranks sixth for my own name. There are four indexed pages on my domain returning 404 — including a blog post about being a fashion model in Shanghai that would be the only content anchoring me to my current market. My contact page does not exist as a URL. There is no structured data anywhere. Every portfolio image is an unreadable base64 blob as far as any machine is concerned.

This is not a confession. It is a demonstration. The framework does not make exceptions. It reads what is there and scores what it finds. The same structural gaps I diagnose in clients' infrastructure exist in mine — because building the tool and using the tool are two different things, and I have been building.

The fixes are on my list. They are the same three fixes at the end of this page. They take one afternoon. I am publishing this case study before implementing them because the value of this work is in its precision, not its performance.

"The gap is always structural. The question is whether you know where it is."

Three fixes.
One afternoon.

These are not strategic recommendations. They are specific, scoped, executable changes — each with a time estimate and an expected score impact. The only variable is whether they get done.

Fix 01

Deploy JSON-LD
Person schema

A structured data block in the <head> of lucabold.com declaring: name, jobTitle, url, sameAs (agency pages, Instagram, LinkedIn), description, knowsAbout, workLocation. This is the foundational fix — without it, no other infrastructure improvement can fully land. Every AI-assisted discovery system that encounters this site currently finds no structured identity.

≈ 3 hours +5–7 pts Machine-Readability
Fix 02

Add meta description
& OG tags

The copy already exists on the homepage. Apply it to the tags. A 155-character meta description. og:title, og:description, og:image, og:url. Controls every social sharing preview, improves SERP snippet quality, and gives any system reading the page a parseable positioning statement before any visual element loads.

≈ 1 hour +2–3 pts Machine-Readability
Fix 03

Redirect the
indexed dead pages

301 redirects from all four indexed 404 URLs to the homepage. /fashion-model, /blogs, /blogs/f/being-a-fashion-model-in-shanghai, /virtual-styling-sessions — all redirect to /. Stops domain authority bleeding to dead pages. Removes the trust failures at SERP positions #4 and #7.

≈ 2 hours +2–3 pts Discoverability
